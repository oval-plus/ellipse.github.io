1 Suppose we wish to test $H_0$ :$\mu=10$ versus $H_1$ :$\mu\ \not= 10$ based on a random sample of size 25. $X_1,X_2,X_3…X_{25}$ from N($\mu,4^2$)
(a) With the significance level α=0.05, what is the rejection region?
(b) What is the power of the test when the true mean is $\mu =12$ ?
(c) Suppose you want to increase the power at $\mu =12$ to be at least 0.8. How large should the sample size be?

(a)rejection region:
$|Z_{obs}|=|\frac{\bar{x_{obs}}-\mu}{\sigma/n}|\ge Z_{α/2}=1.96$
solve to: $\bar{x}\ge11.568\ or\ \bar{x}\le8.432$
(b)$Power\ =\ 1-P(type\ 2\ error)=P(H_0|H_1)=P(reject\ H_0|H_1)$
$=P(\bar{x}\ge11.568|\mu=12)+P(\bar{x}\le8.432|\mu=12)$
$=Pr(\frac{\bar{x}-12}{4/5}\ge\frac{11.568-12}{4/5})+Pr(\frac{\bar{x}-12}{4/5}\le\frac{8.432-12}{4/5})
=1-\Phi(-0.54)+\Phi(-4.46)
=0.7054=c$
(c) $\Phi(-4.46)<–1.96-\frac{\sqrt{n}}{\sigma}$

2. Suppose the lifetime (in hours) of BRITE brand light bulb has the distribution function of the form
F(x)=0,if x≤0；x/750,if 0＜x＜750; 1, if x≥750
A quality assurance imspector of the company randomly sampled 10 bulbs from a large lot and recroded the lifetimes of these bulbs. If the distribution model is correct, what is the longest lifetime of the sampled bulbs that the inspector would expect?

$X_1,X_2,X_3,…,X_{10}$
$F(X_{(10)})=F_u(u)=P(U\ge u)=(\frac{u}{750})^{10}$
$f(X_{(10)})=F^{‘}(X_{(10)})=10\frac{u^9}{750^{10}}$
$E(u)=\int^{750}_{0}uf(u)du=681.82$

3. According to building code in a certain city, elevator are allowed to carry maximum 16 passengers and the maximum load is 2500 pounds. Suppose the average weight of people who use the elevator is 145 pounds, and the standard deviation is 30 pounds. What is the probability that the combined weight of 16 people exceeds the maximum load?
$1-\Phi(1.5)$

4. A fair coin is rolled four times. Let X be the number of heads and let Y be the number of heads minus the number of tails.
(a) Find the joint distribution of (X,Y)
(b) Compute the covariance of X and Y

(a){X:#H}
$X\sim B(4,0.5), Y=X-(4-X)=2X-4$
$P(X=x, Y=y)=P(amount \ of \ H=X,amount \ of \ T=X-Y)=4Cx(0.5)^x(0.5)^{(4-x)}=4Cx(0.5)^4$

(b) $cov(X,Y)=cov(H,H-T)=cov(H,2H-4)=cov(H,2H)-cov(H,4)$
$=cov(H,2H)=2cov(H,H)=2var(H)=2 \times 4\times0.5\times0.5=2$

5. Let $X_1,X_2,…,X_n$be a random sample from a density function of the form
$f(x)=\frac{2(\theta-x)}{(\theta-1)^2} \ for \ 1<x<\theta$
(a) Find the method of moments estimator $\hat{\theta}$ for $\theta$
(b) Find the asymptotic distribution of $\hat{\theta}$

(a) $E(x)=\int^{\theta}_{1}xf(x)dx=\frac{\theta+2}{3}$
$\hat{\theta}=3\bar{x}-2$
(b) $var(x)=\frac{(\theta-1)^2}{18}$
$\sqrt{n}\frac{\hat{\theta}-\theta}{\sqrt{\frac{(\theta-1)^2}{2}}} \sim N(0,1)$

6. Suppose we take two independent observations from a Bernoulli distribution b(1,p). Furthermore, we already know that $1/4\le p \le 3/4$.
(a) Find the mle $\hat{p}$
(b) Consider a (trivial)estimator $p_1 = 1/2$. Compute the relative efficiency of the mle $\hat{p}$ with respect to $p_1$ when the true parameter is p =2/3

(a) $f(x)=p^x(1-p)^{(1-x)}$
$L(p|x)=\prod_{i=1}^{2}f(x_i)=p^{x_1+x_2}(1-p)^{2-(x_1+x_2)}$
$\hat{p}=\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}=\bar{x}$
$\begin{cases}
x_1=x_2=1 &\Rightarrow \hat{p}=3/4\
x_1=x_2=0 &\Rightarrow \hat{p}=1/4\
x_1+x_2=1 &\Rightarrow \hat{p}=1/2\
\end{cases}$
$\hat{p}=\frac{2\bar{x}+1}{4}$
(b) $MSE(p_1)=E(1/2-2/3)^2=1/36$
$MSE(\hat{p})=E(\hat{p}-p)^2=E(\frac{2\bar{x}+1}{4}-2/3)^2$
$=E(\frac{x_1+x_2+1}{4}-2/3)^2=E(\frac{3(x_1+x_2)-5}{12})^2$
5/144
